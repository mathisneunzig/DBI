\begin{frame}{Functional Dependencies}
  \begin{block}{Definition}
    A \textbf{functional dependency (FD)} $A \to B$ holds in a relation if,
    whenever two tuples agree on the value(s) of $A$, they must also agree on
    the value(s) of $B$.  We say ``$A$ \emph{functionally determines} $B$''.
  \end{block}

  \begin{columns}[t]
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
      \textbf{Notation}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item $A \to B$ --- ``A determines B''\\
              Knowing $A$'s value fixes $B$'s value exactly.
        \item $AB \to C$ --- ``A and B together determine C''\\
              Both attributes on the left are required.
        \item $A \twoheadrightarrow B$ --- ``A multi-determines B''\\
              $A$ determines a \emph{set} of $B$ values (multivalued dependency,
              covered in 4NF).
      \end{itemize}
    \end{column}
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
      \textbf{Examples}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item $\texttt{StudentID} \to \texttt{StudentName}$\\
              Each student ID maps to exactly one name.
        \item $\texttt{CourseID} \to \texttt{CourseName}$\\
              Each course ID maps to exactly one name.
        \item $\texttt{AlbumID} \to \texttt{Title, MainArtist, Genre, Release}$\\
              The album ID determines all other album-level attributes.
        \item $\texttt{AlbumID, Song} \to \texttt{FeaturedArtist}$\\
              The combination of album and song determines the featured artist.
      \end{itemize}
    \end{column}
  \end{columns}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Types of Functional Dependencies}
  Understanding the \emph{type} of an FD is essential for knowing which normal
  form it violates and how to fix it.

  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Trivial FD:} The dependent is a subset of the determinant.
          Example: $AB \to B$.  Always true by definition; carries no
          information about the data.
    \item \textbf{Non-trivial FD:} The dependent is \emph{not} part of the
          determinant.  Example: $A \to B$ where $B \notin A$.  These are the
          interesting dependencies to analyse.
    \item \textbf{Fully Functional FD:} $AB \to C$ holds, and \emph{neither}
          $A \to C$ nor $B \to C$ alone holds.  Both $A$ and $B$ are
          genuinely needed.
    \item \textbf{Partial FD:} $AB \to C$ holds, but also $A \to C$ alone
          holds.  $B$ is redundant in the determinant.  Partial FDs are the
          target of 2NF.
    \item \textbf{Transitive FD:} $A \to B$ and $B \to C$ implies
          $A \to C$ transitively.  The attribute $C$ does not depend
          \emph{directly} on $A$.  Transitive FDs are the target of 3NF.
    \item \textbf{Multivalued FD:} $A \twoheadrightarrow B$ --- knowing $A$
          determines an entire \emph{set} of $B$ values, independently of other
          attributes.  Multivalued FDs are the target of 4NF.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
